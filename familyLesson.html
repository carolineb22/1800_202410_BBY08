Family

  • elhtách = parents
  • chésha7 = mother
  • man = father
  • men̓ = child
  • mén̓men = children
  • áyish = opposite-sex sibling
  • kwúpits = older sibling/cousin
  • kwepkwúpits = older siblings
  • si7l = grandparent
  • ímats = grandchild
  • kwtams = husband
  • chewásh = wife

Words in Action

My kwúpits, Paul, refused to play with me, so I told on him to my chésha7. She grabbed him by the ear and scolded him. She then went back to the living room, where my man was listening to the news. She complained to him that the mén̓men were a handful to manage to which he nodded in silent agreement. He then called my si7l, who agreed to take her naughty ímats for the day. Paul wasn't that sad when he heard the news. In fact, he smiled, because he got to hang out with his kwepkwúpits, who were much older than him, some of whom even had a kwtams or a chewásh.



Check Your Understanding (Answer in Squamish)
  1. Takes on this role after carrying a baby for about nine months.
  2. These people are a comfort to the eye for the old, who have already taken care of their own children.
