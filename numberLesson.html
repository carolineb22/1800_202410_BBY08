Words in Action

Counting in Squamish, believe it or not, is similar to counting in English. We start with the first ten numbers starting from nch’u7 to úpen. Then, to move on to the next úpen numbers, we just have to keep "úpen", add "i kwi" to it, and then tack on one of first ten numbers depending on what the number ends in. For example, let's say we want to say 17 in Squamish. We keep "úpen", add "i kwi" to it, and just like in English, because we are dealing with a number that ends in t’akw’usách, our final result is úpen i kwi t’akw’usách. This pattern remains for wetl’ch’, lhéxwlhsha7, x̱wutsnalhshá7, and on. Just like English!
