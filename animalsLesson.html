Words in Action

I thought that my push was interesting until I embraced the outdoors. I wasn't aware of the majestic ch’ésḵen gliding in the air with pride nor the observant chíyatmixw perched up on a branch, silently awaiting prey. The élhḵay̓ in its cunningness slithers almost unseen in the meadows, looking to deceive the unsuspecting kw’át’an or hew̓ít. The smaller and more evasive prey, the ḵwen̓ímach, attends its nightly dinner on the forearms of farmers tending cattle and other animals, such as the kweshú.
